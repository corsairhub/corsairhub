What are the best local multiplayer games on PS4? The truth is, there are so many great options for some couch multiplayer goodness on PS4. While many titles offer great online experiences, nothing quite beats local multiplayer together with friends and family in the same room. Finding the right game can quickly turn four best mates into mortal enemies, or transform the group into a hysterical mess of laughter, screams, and joy.<\/p>\n

Luckily, the PS4 has a plethora of such titles to play. From stone-cold classics like Rayman Legends and Shovel Knight to utterly unique hidden gems such as Cuphead, there’s a wide range of titles to play with others that’ll get everyone leaping out of their seats with excitement.<\/p>\n

As with all these lists, though, it’s not up to us. All the games featured here are chosen and put in order by you lot. Your user ratings directly impact this page, making it an evolving reflection of your favourites. All you need to do is use the search bar below to find some PS4 local multiplayer games, then click the star and rate them accordingly.<\/p>\n

30. Resident Evil 5 (PS4)
\nResident Evil 5 (PS4)
\nResident Evil 5 (PS4)
\n7.27 REVIEW 6\/10 PROFILE
\nPublisher: Capcom \/ Developer: Capcom
\nRelease Date: 28th Jun 2016 (USA) \/ 28th Jun 2016 (UK\/EU)<\/p>\n

Resident Evil 5 was divisive when it released and continues to split opinion today, but co-op shooters don\u2019t really get much more satisfying than Chris Redfield and Sheva Alomar\u2019s shuffle through West Africa. While the game lacked the atmosphere of its immediate predecessor, its PS4 port runs at a silky smooth framerate in 1080p, and bundles in all previously released DLC, making for a lengthy and undeniably entertaining entry in the series.<\/p>\n

29. Street Fighter V (PS4)
\nStreet Fighter V (PS4)
\nStreet Fighter V (PS4)
\n7.31 REVIEW 9\/10 PROFILE
\nPublisher: Capcom \/ Developer: Capcom
\nRelease Date: 16th Feb 2016 (USA) \/ 16th Feb 2016 (UK\/EU)<\/p>\n

Improved dramatically over the years, culminating in the unmissable Street Fighter V: Arcade Edition, few games deliver multiplayer thrills and spills quite like Street Fighter V. This game has benefitted from years of updates and improvements, and has blossomed into a must-play entry in the genre \u2013 despite its day one woes. With a robust spread of single player content and one of the strongest competitive scenes on PS4, this is a total knockout.<\/p>\n

28. Knack 2 (PS4)
\nKnack 2 (PS4)
\nKnack 2 (PS4)
\n7.4 REVIEW 8\/10 PROFILE
\nPublisher: Sony Computer Entertainment \/ Developer: Japan Studio
\nRelease Date: 5th Sep 2017 (USA) \/ 5th Sep 2017 (UK\/EU)<\/p>\n

Knack gets a lot of grief, and has been reduced to little more than memes at this point, but the sequel is a genuinely good action platformer. While the first game undoubtedly has its issues, Knack 2 is a big improvement. Expanded combat options, a bigger emphasis on platforming, and local co-op make this adventure a very enjoyable one that’s suitable for the whole family.<\/p>\n

27. Dragon Ball XenoVerse 2 (PS4)
\nDragon Ball XenoVerse 2 (PS4)
\nDragon Ball XenoVerse 2 (PS4)
\n7.44 REVIEW 8\/10 PROFILE
\nPublisher: Bandai Namco \/ Developer: Dimps
\nRelease Date: 25th Oct 2016 (USA) \/ 28th Oct 2016 (UK\/EU)
\nAvailable On: PS+ Extra<\/p>\n

A mission-based action RPG aimed squarely at Dragon Ball fans, Dragon Ball XenoVerse 2 tasks you with correcting franchise history. Playing as your own custom hero, you travel to various points in the Dragon Ball timeline, helping Goku and the gang out in their iconic battles. Highly customisable combat and a near endless list of missions make XenoVerse 2 an adaptation that’s hard to put down once you’re in the grind.<\/p>\n

26. Injustice: Gods Among Us Ultimate Edition (PS4)
\nInjustice: Gods Among Us Ultimate Edition (PS4)
\nInjustice: Gods Among Us Ultimate Edition (PS4)
\n7.45 REVIEW 8\/10 PROFILE
\nPublisher: Warner Bros \/ Developer: NetherRealm Studios
\nRelease Date: 12th Nov 2013 (USA) \/ 29th Nov 2013 (UK\/EU)<\/p>\n

A souped-up re-release which, bizarrely, Sony bought as a console exclusive to complement the PS4\u2019s launch lineup. Injustice: Gods Among Us Ultimate Edition was, effectively, a port of the PS3’s Injustice: Gods Among Us \u2013 bundling in all of its add-on packs and extras. With a huge cast, spanning DC Comics icons like Batman and Wonder Woman through to more obscure, lesser-known names, this licensed fighter proved a worthy content-packed alternative to the traditional stalwarts of the genre, like Street Fighter.<\/p>\n

25. Child of Light (PS4)
\nChild of Light (PS4)
\nChild of Light (PS4)
\n7.48 REVIEW 9\/10 PROFILE
\nPublisher: Ubisoft \/ Developer: Ubisoft
\nRelease Date: 29th Apr 2014 (USA) \/ 30th Apr 2014 (UK\/EU)
\nAvailable On: PS+ Extra<\/p>\n

Billed as a playable poem, Child of Light is a gorgeous RPG that combines a painterly art style with rhyming narration and an engrossing story. Still unique today, you can play this lovely game alone or with a buddy. Either way, there’s so much to enjoy here, with enjoyable combat and so many wonderful sights and sounds along the journey.<\/p>\n

24. Borderlands: The Handsome Collection (PS4)
\nBorderlands: The Handsome Collection (PS4)
\nBorderlands: The Handsome Collection (PS4)
\n7.57 REVIEW 8\/10 PROFILE
\nPublisher: 2K Games \/ Developer: Gearbox Software
\nRelease Date: 24th Mar 2015 (USA) \/ 27th Mar 2015 (UK\/EU)
\nAvailable On: PS+ Premium<\/p>\n

If you’re after some co-op FPS action, the Borderlands games offer some top notch shooting and looting. Borderlands: The Handsome Collection is a great deal, giving you access to Borderlands 2 and Borderlands: The Pre-Sequel, along with all the accompanying DLC. With a ludicrous number of possible weapons, some great sci-fi environments to explore, and some daft storytelling to pull you along, this series is a highly entertaining way to get your co-op FPS kicks.<\/p>\n

23. Resident Evil: Revelations 2 (PS4)
\nResident Evil: Revelations 2 (PS4)
\nResident Evil: Revelations 2 (PS4)
\n7.58 REVIEW 8\/10 PROFILE
\nPublisher: Capcom \/ Developer: Capcom
\nRelease Date: 18th Mar 2015 (USA) \/ 20th Mar 2015 (UK\/EU)<\/p>\n

Originally released episodically as a direct sequel to Nintendo 3DS game Resident Evil: Revelations, it\u2019s probably fair to say that Resident Evil: Revelations 2 has largely been forgotten. But the game, starring Claire Redfield and Barry Burton\u2019s daughter Moira has some high-points, including full co-op support. When playing solo, you\u2019ll need to toggle between different characters to solve puzzles. There\u2019s also a Raid Mode which features a bunch of combat gauntlets, all of which can be enjoyed with a friend locally and online.<\/p>\n

22. Overcooked (PS4)
\nOvercooked (PS4)
\nOvercooked (PS4)
\n7.58 REVIEW 8\/10 PROFILE
\nPublisher: Team17 \/ Developer: Ghost Town Games
\nRelease Date: 2nd Aug 2016 (USA) \/ 2nd Aug 2016 (UK\/EU)
\nDespite later versions improving upon the formula, the original Overcooked is still a wonderful multiplayer game. It kicked off a trend of simple, accessible co-op games, and it’s not hard to understand why. This cooking game may look friendly and straightforward, but if you and your friends aren’t careful, chaos can quickly take over the kitchen. Communication is key in this fantastic co-op game, and if you want a bit of spice, there are some competitive multiplayer levels too.<\/p>\n
